title:          "Seismic Retrofit of Welded Steel Moment Connections with Highly Composite Floor Slabs"
date:           2017-12-15 00:01:00 +0900
selected:       false
pub:            "Journal of Constructional Steel Research"
pub_date:       "2017"
abstract: >-
  In the 1994 Northridge earthquake, connection damage initiated from the beam
  bottom flange was prevalent in welded steel moment frames, and the composite
  action due to the presence of a concrete floor slab was speculated as a
  critical cause of the bottom-flange fracture. Past experimental studies by
  the authors clearly indicated that conventional steel moment connections
  with highly composite slabs are much more vulnerable to bottom-flange
  fracture. Three seismic retrofit schemes are presented for welded steel
  moment connections with highly composite floor slabs typical of existing
  steel moment frames in Korea. Because top-flange modification is infeasible
  in the presence of a slab, beam bottom-flange or web modifications using
  welded triangular or straight haunches and a heavy shear tab were cyclically
  tested. All retrofit schemes effectively eliminated the detrimental effect
  of high composite action and ensured excellent connection plastic rotation
  exceeding 4% rad.
