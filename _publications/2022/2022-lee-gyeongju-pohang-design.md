title:          "Structural Damage Potentials and Design Implications of 2016 Gyeongju and 2017 Pohang Earthquakes in Korea"
date:           2022-03-15 00:01:00 +0900

pub:            "Earthquake and Structures"
pub_date:       "2022"
abstract: >-
  A comparative study of the damage potentials of the 2016 Gyeongju and 2017
  Pohang earthquakes in Korea is presented. Plausible technical explanations
  are provided for the more severe damage observed in the 2017 Pohang event
  despite its relatively weaker magnitude and intensity measures, based on
  the response analysis of elastic and inelastic single-degree-of-freedom
  systems for the recorded ground motions. A detailed case study is also
  conducted for a fatally damaged piloti building with an eccentric shear
  wall core through nonlinear dynamic analysis using ground motions modified
  for the building site.
